Overview

Sarasota is a beautiful Gulf Coast city known for world-class beaches -- Siesta Key has been rated the #1 beach in the United States -- and a strong arts and culture scene anchored by the Ringling Museum, Sarasota Opera, Orchestra, and Ballet. Florida's 0% state income tax is a major financial advantage for remote workers and families alike.

The city has good public schools (A-rated district) and a richer classical education landscape than initially assessed -- five classical options including The Classical Academy of Sarasota, Liberty Christian Academy, Chesterton Academy of St. Padre Pio, Riverstone Classical Academy, and a tuition-free classical charter opening Fall 2026. The biggest concern for a young family is demographics: with a median age of 48.2 and a retiree-driven community culture, Sarasota skews very old. Lakewood Ranch is the standout family-friendly pocket. Reformed church presence is a clear weakness, with only one PCA and one OPC congregation in the area.

Cornerstone PCA in Lakewood Ranch is where the PCA is investing. Founded in 1993 with fewer than 50 people at a strip-mall cinema, it now draws 450 weekly and just completed a $7.7M expansion -- new 10,000 sq ft sanctuary, youth wing, playground, and coffee bar on an 8-acre campus. Senior Pastor Phil Woods (since 2013) explicitly prefers a "boutique church" model over megachurch sprawl, emphasizing intellectual credibility and emotional satisfaction. Two associate pastors -- Rev. Bill Martin (RTS, radio host on The JOY FM, member since 1995) and Rev. Taylor White (Pastor of Liturgy & Theology) -- round out a serious pastoral team. This is the flagship Reformed church in the fastest-growing master-planned community in the country.

The most interesting development for Reformed families. Dr. Steven Jeantet is planting New Creation Church in Skye Ranch -- a 1,000-acre master-planned community targeting young families with homes priced 15-25% below Lakewood Ranch. A new K-8 school opened there in August 2025. The church is still tiny (core group stage) but the strategic logic is sound: a PCA church plant in a young-family development that is growing fast. Jeantet is connected to Covenant Life PCA (effectively a daughter church) and supported by the Florida Church Planting Network -- a collaboration of four PCA presbyteries contributing ~$400,000/year to planters. If you are moving to Skye Ranch, this is your church, and you would be part of building something from the ground up.

Florida's universal voucher program (~$7,700/student via Step Up for Students) is a game-changer for affordability. About half of Classical Academy's students use vouchers. Liberty Christian accepts EdChoice scholarships. Chesterton families pay roughly $2,800 after scholarship support. This makes private Christian education accessible to families who could not afford it in most other states.

The Honest Bottom Line

Sarasota is viable for a young Reformed family but requires intentionality. You will not stumble into a Reformed community here the way you would in Greenville, SC or Grand Rapids, MI. The Reformed community is a minority within a minority -- a small confessional community within a larger evangelical community within a large retiree community. There is no Reformed seminary, no Gospel Coalition local chapter, no cross-church Reformed theology group, and no Reformed bookstore.

But the trajectory is positive. The retiree-oriented metro has a counterweight: Lakewood Ranch and Skye Ranch are young, growing, family-oriented, and exactly where the PCA is investing. Cornerstone PCA is growing 20% annually and just put $7.7M into expansion. New Creation is planting in the path of growth. Classical schools are expanding enrollment. Conservative families continue migrating to Florida. The broader culture is hospitable -- this is a place where conservative Christian values are culturally at home, not countercultural.

The risk is not hostility. The risk is shallowness -- a conservative culture that is politically tribal rather than theologically grounded. The Reformed community provides the depth that generic "conservative Christianity" does not. But you have to seek it out.

Pros and Cons

Strengths

  • 0% state income tax -- significant financial advantage
  • World-class beaches (Siesta Key #1 in US)
  • Strong arts and culture scene (Ringling Museum, Opera, Ballet)
  • A-rated public school district, 93% graduation rate
  • Mild winters (72/52) with 251 sunny days
  • Good equestrian access in Myakka area
  • Lakewood Ranch is an excellent family-friendly pocket
  • High remote work friendliness

Weaknesses

  • Thin Reformed church presence (5 congregations including a PCA plant)
  • Retiree-oriented demographics -- median age 48.2
  • Limited young family community outside Lakewood Ranch
  • Higher housing costs ($485K median, 130 housing index)
  • Hot, humid summers (89/79) with hurricane risk
  • Higher cost of living overall (index 105)
  • Higher crime rates (592 violent, 2,749 property per 100K)
  • Classical school options improving (5+ schools) but several are new and small
