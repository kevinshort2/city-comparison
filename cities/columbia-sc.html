Overview

Columbia is the capital of South Carolina and home to the University of South Carolina, giving it the character of a university town layered onto state government. It is the most affordable city in this comparison, with a median home price of $271K and a cost of living index right at the national average (99).

What sets Columbia apart is its surprisingly strong PCA presence: 18+ churches across the metro area, the highest reformed church density of any city in this study. Northeast Presbyterian, Christ Covenant, St. Andrews, and the historic Rose Hill Presbyterian (est. 1919) anchor a deep reformed community. Columbia International University and Erskine Seminary's extension campus add theological infrastructure.

Summers are hot and humid with highs reaching 92F, but winters are mild (55F highs, rarely below freezing). The suburbs -- Lexington SC, Chapin, and Blythewood -- are family-friendly with strong schools and low crime. The Aiken equestrian area, a world-class horse community, sits about an hour southwest.

Christian Community Deep Dive

25+ PCA churches in Palmetto Presbytery, two seminaries, and a nationally significant ARP pulpit -- one of the densest Reformed ecosystems in the Southeast

Key Pastors & Churches

The Palmetto Presbytery covers the Columbia metro (Richland, Lexington, Aiken, Fairfield, Kershaw counties) with 24 particularized churches and 2 mission churches. Add Korean-language PCA congregations and the total reaches 28+. These are the anchor congregations.

ARP Presence -- Nationally Significant

First Presbyterian Church (ARP) on Marion Street in downtown Columbia is one of the most historically significant Reformed congregations in the United States. Dr. Sinclair B. Ferguson -- one of the most influential Reformed theologians alive, a Scottish-born Ligonier Teaching Fellow -- served as Senior Minister until his retirement in 2013. Dr. Derek W. H. Thomas, RTS Chancellor's Professor and Ligonier Teaching Fellow, succeeded Ferguson and served until his retirement in December 2023.

The current Senior Minister is Dr. Neil C. Stewart, supported by a deep staff: Dr. David Lauten (Congregational Care), Rev. Bryan Bult (Seniors), Rev. David Henderson (Discipleship), Rev. Sean Henderson (Missions), and Rev. Josh Squires (Counseling).

The ARP denomination has deep roots in South Carolina. Additional ARP congregations in the Columbia area include Centennial ARP Church (est. 1896, mother church of several other ARP congregations) and Sherwood Forest ARP Church.

The Ferguson and Thomas legacy means Columbia has been a destination for Reformed theology for over two decades. Combined with 25+ PCA churches and two seminaries, this creates a Reformed theological density that few cities in the Southeast can match.

Seminary & Institutional Pipeline

Two seminary institutions in Columbia feed pastoral talent directly into area churches and the broader Reformed world.

Columbia International University
3,239
students (largest freshman class in 102-year history)

President: Dr. William H. Jones. 400-acre campus. Columbia Biblical Seminary offers M.Div., MA, D.Min., and PhD programs. Direct pipeline to local PCA pulpits -- Heritage PCA pastor Jim Schirmer holds his M.Div. and MA in OT from CIU. Seminary students serve in area congregations during their studies. Strong missions emphasis feeds into the missionary work of churches like Rose Hill PCA.

Erskine Theological Seminary
Columbia
campus at 1420 Lady Street (downtown)

The seminary of the Associate Reformed Presbyterian Church, offering complete degree programs at its Columbia campus since 2010: M.Div., MA in Practical Ministry, MA in Theological Studies, Th.M., and D.Min. Residential, blended, and fully online formats. Primarily serves the ARP denomination but draws students from across Reformed traditions, supporting the pastoral pipeline to First Presbyterian ARP and other area congregations.

How to Get Involved

A step-by-step newcomer guide for a Reformed family relocating to the Columbia metro.

Step Action Details
1 Choose your suburb Chapin (lake, single strong PCA church), Lexington (most options, most affordable), or Blythewood (fast-growing, young families). This choice largely determines your church home.
2 Visit 2-3 PCA churches Start with the anchor church in your suburb. Use the PCA Church Finder at pcaac.org or the Palmetto Presbytery directory.
3 Visit First Presbyterian ARP Even if you settle on a PCA church, attend at least one service here to experience the pulpit that hosted Ferguson and Thomas. 1324 Marion Street, downtown.
4 Connect with homeschool networks Most PCA churches have homeschool families who can connect you to local Classical Conversations communities, co-ops, and resources. Covenant Classical Christian School (K4-12) is the PCA-connected school option.
5 Join cross-church networks Palmetto Presbytery Women's Ministries (palmettopreswm.com) coordinates women's ministry across all 25+ PCA churches. Focused Living serves men in the Midlands. FCA Midlands covers 15 counties for families in athletics.
6 Attend a Presbytery meeting Palmetto Presbytery meets regularly. Attending is the fastest way to see the broader PCA network, meet pastors from across the metro, and understand how the churches connect.
7 Explore CIU events Columbia International University hosts public lectures, chapel services, and conferences open to the community. Ben Lippen School (CIU-affiliated, PK-12) is another Christian school option.

Pros and Cons

Advantages

  • Most affordable housing of all eight cities ($271K median)
  • Strongest PCA church density in the comparison (25+ churches in Palmetto Presbytery)
  • Cost of living right at national average (99 index)
  • Very low property tax on primary residence ($1,800/yr on $400K)
  • Covenant Classical Christian School (ACCS, 1210-1240 SAT, 100% grad rate)
  • Lexington-Richland District 5 (Chapin schools) ranked #1 in Midlands
  • Lake Murray (50K acres) for boating and water sports
  • Aiken equestrian area about 1 hour away
  • Short commute times (15 min average)
  • CIU and Erskine Seminary add theological depth

Disadvantages

  • Hot, humid summers (92F highs, oppressive July-August)
  • Elevated city crime rates (770 violent, 3,233 property per 100K)
  • Lower median household income ($55,653)
  • Walk score of 35 -- entirely car-dependent
  • Hiking is adequate but not a strength (flat terrain)
  • No small-town feel in the city proper
  • State income tax tops at 6%
  • Remote-work friendliness rated only medium
