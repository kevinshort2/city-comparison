Key Takeaways

Chattanooga, TN is the most affordable city overall (index 88.6), narrowly edging out Lexington (90.8) and Greenville (91.3). All three sit well below the national average of 100. Columbia, SC (99.0) is nearly average, while Sarasota (105) and Coeur d'Alene (114) run moderately above. The Oregon cities -- Bend (133) and Sisters (134) -- are the most expensive, driven almost entirely by housing indices near 200.

On taxes, Florida and Tennessee stand out with zero state income tax. Chattanooga offsets this with a high 9.25% sales tax but has the lowest property tax bill ($1,510 on a $400K home). Oregon has no sales tax but imposes steep income tax (up to 9.9%). South Carolina and Idaho fall in the middle with moderate income tax and property tax. Lexington's 3.5% flat income tax and $3,480 property tax on $400K are moderate by comparison.

For remote workers earning an out-of-state income, the no-income-tax states (FL, TN) offer the clearest financial advantage. For families prioritizing overall affordability with a balanced tax picture, Chattanooga, Lexington, and Greenville offer the strongest value.
