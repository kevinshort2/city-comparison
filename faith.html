Key Takeaways

Strongest for Reformed Families

  • Greenville, SC and Chattanooga, TN are both perfect 10s. Each has 20+ Reformed congregations, multiple large PCA churches, seminary or college presence, and vibrant inter-church community life. Either city would place a Reformed family in the heart of American Presbyterianism.
  • Columbia, SC rounds out a powerful Southeast cluster. With 18+ Reformed congregations (6 PCA), two seminary-level institutions, and affordable housing, it is a strong option despite lacking OPC or CREC diversity.
  • The Southeast triangle of Greenville, Chattanooga, and Columbia represents the densest concentration of PCA life in the country. Any of these three cities would be an upgrade over Lexington for a Reformed family.

Weakest / Potential Dealbreakers

  • Sisters, OR scores 1/10 with zero Reformed churches in town. The nearest options require a 25-30 minute drive to Bend, which itself is weak. This is a dealbreaker for a family that prioritizes Reformed church life.
  • Bend, OR scores 2/10. One OPC and one Reformed Baptist plant-stage congregation. No PCA, no CREC, no seminary. Progressive culture makes Reformed families a small minority.
  • Sarasota, FL scores 3/10 with thin Reformed presence. Two PCA churches (Covenant Life plus the New Creation plant in Skye Ranch) and one OPC serve the metro, but the retiree-oriented demographics limit young family networks.
  • Lexington, KY (baseline) and Coeur d'Alene, ID both score 5/10 — functional but limited. Lexington has two solid PCA congregations but no seminary. CDA has a new PCA plant and benefits from the Moscow CREC ecosystem 84 miles south.
