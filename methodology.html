Methodology

How the scores were calculated, what criteria drive each category, and where the underlying data came from.

Scoring System

Each city is scored from 1 to 10 across six categories. These category scores are combined into a single weighted average that represents overall fit for the Short family's priorities.

The six categories and their default weights are:

Category Weight Rationale
Faith 25% Access to Reformed churches is a non-negotiable filter for the family.
Education 25% Classical Christian schooling and strong homeschool communities are essential.
Lifestyle 20% Outdoor recreation, equestrian access, climate, and safety matter significantly.
Housing 15% Affordable homes and ranch/acreage availability are important but secondary to faith and education.
Cost of Living 10% Lower cost of living is preferred, but the family can absorb moderate differences.
Community 5% Family-friendliness and conservative community presence are valued but less decisive.

The overall score is calculated as: (Faith x 0.25) + (Education x 0.25) + (Lifestyle x 0.20) + (Housing x 0.15) + (Cost of Living x 0.10) + (Community x 0.05). Weights are adjustable via the sliders on the Overview page.

Category Scoring Criteria

What factors determine each city's 1-10 score in each category.

Faith

Number of Reformed churches (PCA, OPC, EPC, CREC) within reasonable driving distance. Presence of Reformed seminaries or theological institutions. Overall Christian community vitality and opportunities for fellowship, church planting, and conference culture.

Education

Availability and quality of classical Christian schools (especially ACCS-accredited). Strength and size of the homeschool community, including co-ops and Classical Conversations chapters. Public school ratings provide context but are weighted less heavily given the family's preference for classical Christian education.

Lifestyle

Quality and variety of outdoor recreation (hiking, biking, skiing, water sports). Equestrian access and horse-friendly culture. Climate desirability across all four seasons. Safety as measured by violent and property crime rates.

Housing

Median home price relative to national average and household income. Availability of ranch and acreage properties (5+ acres). Horse-friendly zoning and rural land accessibility within a reasonable commute.

Cost of Living

Overall cost-of-living index (lower index = better score). State income tax rate and effective property tax rate. Everyday costs including groceries, utilities, and transportation.

Community

Family-friendliness as reflected by demographics, schools, and amenities. Homeschool community size and engagement. Conservative and faith-community presence. Population growth trends (growing communities tend to offer more opportunity).

Caveats

All data was gathered in March 2026. Housing markets, cost-of-living indices, church directories, and school enrollment figures change over time. These scores represent a snapshot, not a permanent ranking.

Personal visits are essential before making a relocation decision. No amount of desk research can substitute for walking a neighborhood, attending a church service, touring a school, or talking to local families.

These scores reflect one family's priorities. The weighting system is deliberately opinionated toward Reformed faith and classical Christian education. A different family with different priorities would produce a very different ranking.
