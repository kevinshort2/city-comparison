Land Profiles by City

Lexington, KY

The Horse Capital of the World. Surrounding counties (Woodford, Scott, Jessamine, Madison) offer abundant 10+ acre parcels at $6K-$30K per acre. This is one of the best places in the country for equestrian land -- horse-friendly zoning is the norm, not the exception. Southern Fayette County provides equestrian estates with rural character while remaining minutes from city amenities.

Chattanooga, TN

The Sequatchie Valley (30-45 min west) offers the best ranch value at $12K-$20K per acre, with parcels of 10+ acres regularly available. Soddy-Daisy provides more affordable horse property closer to town. Hamilton County zoning accommodates livestock on larger lots throughout the unincorporated areas.

Greenville, SC

Easley and Pickens County to the west, plus Fountain Inn and Laurens County to the south, offer the most affordable acreage at $9K-$25K per acre. The agricultural character of these areas makes horse ownership straightforward. Tryon, NC (a major equestrian hub) is only 45 minutes away, adding to the equestrian infrastructure.

Columbia, SC

Newberry and outer Lexington County provide good ranch land at $10K-$54K per acre, with the lower end of that range available in more rural areas. Lake Murray suburbs like Chapin offer some larger lots. The Aiken equestrian district, one of the premier horse communities in the Southeast, is about an hour away.

Bend, OR

The cheapest raw acreage of any city on the list -- Crook and Jefferson counties start around $2K per acre for large parcels. Tumalo and rural Bend offer horse properties with larger lots. The Deschutes National Forest provides extensive equestrian trail access. The trade-off is that home prices in Bend proper are among the highest.

Sisters, OR

Cascade Meadow Ranch is a premier equestrian community with smaller lots. For larger ranch parcels, the Tumalo area between Sisters and Bend is the best option but comes at $8K-$48K per acre. Overall land availability is limited due to the tiny market and surrounding federal land. The Sisters Rodeo and national forest trail access are strong equestrian draws.

Sarasota, FL

Myakka City and east Sarasota County offer rural acreage at $35K-$54K per acre -- the most expensive ranch land on the list. Horse-friendly zoning exists in these areas but inventory is limited and prices are elevated. The 30-45 minute drive to downtown Sarasota is the trade-off for rural living. Private equestrian communities like Little Manatee provide options.

Coeur d'Alene, ID

Dalton Gardens (a semi-rural enclave with 1-acre minimum lots) allows horses and sits close to town. Rathdrum and Post Falls offer larger parcels. Bonner County to the north is significantly cheaper than Kootenai County. National forest horse trails are abundant. Land prices range from $22K-$47K per acre, placing it in the upper-middle tier.

Flagstaff, AZ

Doney Park and Bellemont offer the most affordable acreage near Flagstaff at $15K-$75K per acre, with A-R (Agricultural Residential) zoning that permits horses on 1+ acre lots. Williams, 30 minutes west, drops to $10K-$50K per acre. Overall land availability is limited due to surrounding Coconino National Forest and high demand. The millions of acres of national forest provide extensive equestrian trail access despite the tight private land market.

Birmingham, AL

Shelby County south of Birmingham offers the best ranch land at $15K-$30K per acre, with horse-friendly zoning and parcels of 10+ acres regularly available. St. Clair County to the east provides additional options. The Oak Mountain area combines suburban amenities with larger lots suitable for equestrian use. Alabama's low property tax rate (0.45%) makes land ownership very affordable long-term.

Moscow, ID

The rolling Palouse farmland surrounding Moscow provides affordable ranch parcels at $10K-$17K per acre. Latah County offers good availability of 10+ acre lots with horse-friendly zoning. The rural character of the area makes livestock ownership straightforward. The tradeoff is that Moscow is a small college town (U of Idaho) with limited urban amenities, though the strong CREC community provides rich social infrastructure.

Raleigh, NC

Chatham County to the west and Johnston/Harnett Counties to the south offer ranch land at $15K-$70K per acre, with the lower end available in more rural areas. Horse-friendly zoning exists throughout the surrounding counties. The wide price range reflects the proximity to the booming Research Triangle metro. Areas closer to town command premium prices, while more rural parcels 30-45 minutes out remain affordable.

Sheridan, WY

Exceptional horse-friendly culture with polo clubs, dude ranches, and the Big Horn Mountains as a backdrop. Ranch land in Sheridan County runs $8K-$12K per acre with good availability of large parcels. Wyoming's low property tax (0.64%) and zero state income tax make land ownership very affordable long-term. The Big Horn foothills provide stunning ranch settings within minutes of town.

Boerne, TX

Texas Hill Country offers excellent horse-friendly land at $15K-$35K per acre. Kendall County has strong equestrian culture with ranch properties regularly available. No state income tax and relatively low property taxes on rural land make ownership attractive. The area between Boerne and Comfort provides the best ranch value within 15-20 minutes of town.

Bozeman, MT

Gallatin County ranch land prices vary widely from $10K per acre for remote parcels to $100K+ near town. Horse-friendly zoning exists throughout the county. Montana's Big Sky country provides stunning mountain backdrops for ranch living. The trade-off is extreme winter conditions and very high home prices in Bozeman proper ($745K median).

Boise, ID

Land surrounding Boise ranges from $25K to $100K+ per acre depending on proximity to town. Canyon County to the west and Owyhee County to the south offer more affordable parcels with horse-friendly zoning. Idaho's low property tax (0.66%) makes land ownership affordable long-term. The Boise foothills provide scenic ranch settings but at premium prices.

Key Takeaways

Most affordable housing: Columbia, SC stands out with a median home price of $271K -- roughly 60% less than the Oregon cities. Chattanooga ($335K) and Lexington ($330K) follow closely and also carry low property tax burdens, with Chattanooga offering a very low property tax at $2,240 on a $400K home (0.56% unincorporated Hamilton County rate).

Best land availability: Lexington, KY earns an "Excellent" rating for land availability and offers the strongest horse-friendly infrastructure in the country. Its surrounding counties provide 10+ acre parcels starting at just $6K per acre. Bend, OR has surprisingly cheap raw acreage in neighboring Crook and Jefferson counties (from $2K/acre), though home prices in town are prohibitive.

Best combination of affordable homes and available acreage: Chattanooga, Greenville, and Lexington offer the strongest overall package. All three have median home prices under $350K, ranch land priced at $6K-$25K per acre, horse-friendly zoning, and good-to-excellent land availability. Among these, Lexington has the edge for equestrian families, while Chattanooga offers the lowest overall cost with no state income tax. Greenville provides the best balance of affordable surrounding-county land with proximity to a vibrant, growing metro.
