Homeschool Community Strength

Comparing homeschool co-ops, Classical Conversations availability, legal environment, and support networks in each city.

Chattanooga, TN

Rating: Very Strong

3 Classical Conversations communities (Signal Mountain, East Brainerd, Ooltewah). Additional co-ops include Chattanooga Classical Beginnings (1st-12th, Mon-Thu), CSTHEA support association, and Nickajack Homeschool Co-op. Tennessee requires notice of intent and annual attendance records only.

Greenville, SC

Rating: Very Strong

Multiple Classical Conversations communities throughout Upstate SC. Artios Academy (hybrid integrated curriculum), Milton Academy (college-prep middle/high), and Upcountry Homeschool Association provide extensive co-op and tutoring options. South Carolina requires accountability association membership or approved homeschool association.

Columbia, SC

Rating: Strong

Classical Conversations active in multiple Midlands communities. HERO (Home Educator's Resource Organization) in Irmo, GROUNDED co-op (K-9th at Riverland Hills Baptist), and Grace Community Academy (part-time high school). Same SC accountability association requirement as Greenville.

Lexington, KY

Rating: Strong

Classical Conversations present with multiple communities. Christian Home Educators of the Bluegrass (CHEB) is a well-established support network. Heritage Homeschool Fellowship offers PreK-12 supplemental classes and clubs. Christian Homeschool of the Commonwealth (CHC) provides additional co-op options. Kentucky requires notification to the local school superintendent only.

Coeur d'Alene, ID

Rating: Very Strong

Classical Conversations communities active in the CDA area. Inland Northwest Christian Homeschoolers (INCH) provides regional support. CDA Homeschool Community covers CDA, Hayden, Rathdrum, and Post Falls. Idaho is one of the most homeschool-friendly states with no notification or assessment requirements.

Sarasota, FL

Rating: Moderate

Classical Conversations present in Bradenton, Sarasota, and Venice. Florida Parent Educators Association (FPEA) provides statewide support groups. Florida requires annual portfolio review or standardized testing. Community is present but less concentrated due to retiree-dominant demographics.

Bend, OR

Rating: Moderate

No Classical Conversations in Bend. Cascade Christian Co-op offers supplementary classes, social events, and field trips. Uplift Central Oregon provides general homeschool support and enrichment. Oregon requires notification to local ESD and annual assessment. Smaller community relative to city size.

Sisters, OR

Rating: Moderate

No Classical Conversations in Sisters. Shares Cascade Christian Co-op with Bend. Commonplace Sisters offers art, music, and drama enrichment classes locally. Same Oregon notification and assessment requirements. Very small pool of homeschool families given the town's population of 2,899.

Flagstaff, AZ

Rating: Moderate

Limited Classical Conversations presence in the Flagstaff area. Arizona is a homeschool-friendly state requiring only an affidavit of intent filed with the county superintendent. NAU student population skews demographics but the permanent family community supports a moderate homeschool network. Smaller pool relative to city size due to university-town transience.

Birmingham, AL

Rating: Strong

4-6 Classical Conversations communities in the Birmingham metro area. Strong homeschool network in Shelby County and surrounding suburbs. Alabama requires notification only, making it a homeschool-friendly state. Briarwood PCA and Westminster school communities provide additional family networks.

Moscow, ID

Rating: Strong

1 Classical Conversations community. CREC-connected homeschool networks are deeply integrated with Logos School and Christ Church community. Idaho requires no notification or assessment. The intentional CREC community creates strong family support despite the small town size.

Raleigh, NC

Rating: Strong

15+ Classical Conversations communities across the Triangle area. North Carolinians for Home Education (NCHE) provides statewide support. Multiple co-ops and enrichment programs available. North Carolina requires notification and annual testing. Large and active homeschool community reflecting the metro's size.

Sheridan, WY

Rating: Moderate

Wyoming is very homeschool-friendly with notification-only requirements. Moderate homeschool community relative to small population. Limited co-op options locally. Strong family values and supportive community culture compensate for smaller formal networks.

Growth Trends

Population growth affects housing prices, school quality, traffic, and community character. Here is what growth means for each city.

Fast Growth

2.0%+ Annual

Coeur d'Alene, ID (2.3%/year) is the fastest-growing city in this comparison. Driven by American Redoubt migration and remote workers fleeing West Coast cities. Growth is strengthening the conservative and faith community but pushing home prices up rapidly (median $601K). Infrastructure is struggling to keep pace.

Above Average Growth

1.0% - 2.0% Annual

Bend, OR (1.7%) and Greenville, SC (1.5%) are growing briskly. Bend's growth brings outdoor recreation investment but also rising costs and a more transient population. Greenville's growth is strengthening its economy while maintaining its faith and family character. Columbia, SC (1.4%) and Sarasota, FL (1.1%) show steady expansion. Chattanooga, TN (1.0%) is growing at a sustainable pace that supports community stability without overheating housing.

Modest Growth

Below 1.0% Annual

Lexington, KY (0.8%/year) is growing slowly, which keeps housing affordable and maintains the established community character. The university provides economic stability but moderate growth means fewer new families are arriving. Flagstaff, AZ (0.14%/year) is nearly flat, with NAU's student population masking limited permanent family growth.

Declining

Negative Growth

Sisters, OR (-1.0%/year) is the only city losing population. The aging demographics (median age 49.6) and lack of affordable housing for young families are driving this trend. While small-town charm remains, the shrinking population means fewer community resources and a smaller peer group for children.

Key Takeaways

Best Community for a Young Reformed Christian Homeschool Family

Chattanooga, TN and Greenville, SC are tied at the top with community scores of 9/10. Both offer excellent family-friendliness, very strong homeschool communities with multiple Classical Conversations locations, and deeply conservative faith cultures. Chattanooga edges ahead with Covenant College and a tighter-knit Reformed community anchored on Lookout Mountain. Greenville offers a larger metro with more church and school choices.

Strongest Like-Minded Community

Coeur d'Alene, ID also scores 9/10 and deserves special mention. The American Redoubt migration is actively drawing conservative Christian families to the area, creating a community that is self-selecting for shared values. Idaho's minimal homeschool regulation and the growing Moscow CREC ecosystem (84 miles south) add depth. The tradeoff is higher housing costs and harsh winters.

Solid Middle Ground

Lexington, KY (7/10) and Columbia, SC (6/10) offer strong homeschool networks and affordable living but lack the concentrated conservative culture of the top three. Lexington's university presence creates a more ideologically mixed environment. Columbia's strong PCA church network compensates for a less family-oriented city core.

Community Cautions

Sarasota, FL (4/10) and Sisters, OR (4/10) present the weakest community fit. Sarasota's retiree-dominant demographics mean fewer young families and a thinner homeschool network. Sisters' declining population and aging residents (median age 49.6) limit peer opportunities for children. Bend, OR (5/10) has good family amenities but its progressive culture and weak conservative presence make it a harder fit for a Reformed Christian family.
