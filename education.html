Key Takeaways

Strongest for Classical Christian Education

  • Chattanooga has the deepest bench: Veritas Classical hybrid, Candies Creek (ACCS), plus the newer Regents Classical and Point Christian Academy -- four classical options across different models, with 3 Classical Conversations locations and a free classical charter (Skillern at Ivy Academy). Grace Baptist Academy ($4K/yr, SAT avg 1080-1300, 90-96% college acceptance) uses a traditional Christian curriculum, not classical, but is a strong affordable option. The broader Christian school ecosystem is also strong with CCS, Boyd-Buchanan, Silverdale, and Berean.
  • Greenville matches on quality with three classical schools (GCA with ACCS accreditation, Veritas Prep hybrid, and the new King's Classical), a free Ascent Classical charter opening 2026, plus multiple CC communities. Broader private school depth includes Bob Jones Academy, Southside Christian, First Presbyterian Academy, and Christ Church Episcopal.
  • Lexington is stronger than initially scored: Trinity Christian Academy (since 1988, trivium-based), Veritas hybrid, and now Lexington Latin School ($3,280-$6,705, Memoria Press curriculum) give it three classical options at varied price points. LCA adds a large evangelical alternative. Bumped from 7 to 8.
  • Columbia has two classical schools: the exceptional Covenant Classical (SAT avg 1375, 100% graduation rate, ACCS accredited) plus Heritage Christian Academy (ACCS, PK-8 in Lexington suburb), with a free Ascent Classical charter coming 2026.

Weakest / Dealbreaker Concerns

  • Bend, OR has two classical schools: Chesterton Academy of Mater Dei (Catholic, high school only, 8 students) and Immanuel Classical School (Reformed, K-12, covenantal discipleship model). Both are small but growing. Score: 5. Sisters, OR has no classical schools in town but benefits from Bend's options (25 min). Score: 3. Oregon's homeschool laws are more restrictive than southeastern states.
  • Sarasota is richer than initially scored: five classical or classical-adjacent schools now (Classical Academy, Liberty Christian, Chesterton St. Padre Pio, Incarnation Catholic, Riverstone hybrid), plus a free classical charter (Sarasota Classical Prep) opening Fall 2026. FL scholarships reduce effective tuition significantly. Bumped from 6 to 7.
  • Coeur d'Alene now has three classical schools: Classical Christian Academy (founded 1995, ACCS), the Hillsdale-affiliated Kootenai Classical, and Coeur du Christ Academy (Catholic classical, opened 2022). All are smaller but the variety of models is a strength.

Best Homeschool Communities

Tennessee, South Carolina, and Idaho all have very homeschool-friendly laws with minimal regulation. Chattanooga and Greenville combine the best of both worlds: strong classical Christian schools as a primary option, with robust homeschool co-op networks as a supplement or alternative. Coeur d'Alene stands out for Idaho's nearly zero-regulation homeschool laws and a very strong conservative Christian homeschool community. Kentucky is friendly but requires superintendent notification. Florida offers substantial scholarship programs (Step Up, EdChoice) that reduce private school costs dramatically. Oregon requires notification and standardized testing, making it the most regulated of the group.
