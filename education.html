Key Takeaways

Strongest for Classical Christian Education

  • Chattanooga leads with Grace Baptist Academy ($4K/yr, 95th percentile nationally, SAT avg 1380) plus Veritas Classical hybrid option and 3 Classical Conversations locations.
  • Greenville matches with three classical schools (GCA with ACCS accreditation, Veritas Prep hybrid, and the new King's Classical), plus multiple CC communities and three seminaries feeding the pipeline.
  • Columbia now has two classical schools: the exceptional Covenant Classical (SAT avg 1375, 100% graduation rate, ACCS accredited) plus Heritage Christian Academy (ACCS, PK-8 in Lexington suburb), alongside active CC communities.
  • Lexington offers Trinity Christian Academy (since 1988, trivium-based) and Veritas hybrid, with strong homeschool support through CHEB and CC.

Weakest / Dealbreaker Concerns

  • Bend, OR now has one classical school (Chesterton Academy of Mater Dei, Catholic, high school only, 8 students) — still very limited but no longer zero. Sisters, OR remains at zero. Neither has Classical Conversations communities, and Oregon's homeschool laws are more restrictive than southeastern states.
  • Sarasota has options but Liberty Christian Academy is still growing (only through 7th grade) and the Classical Academy's $12,500 tuition is mid-range.
  • Coeur d'Alene has two classical schools but both are smaller; the Hillsdale-affiliated Kootenai Classical is a strong newcomer to watch.

Best Homeschool Communities

Tennessee, South Carolina, and Idaho all have very homeschool-friendly laws with minimal regulation. Chattanooga and Greenville combine the best of both worlds: strong classical Christian schools as a primary option, with robust homeschool co-op networks as a supplement or alternative. Coeur d'Alene stands out for Idaho's nearly zero-regulation homeschool laws and a very strong conservative Christian homeschool community. Kentucky is friendly but requires superintendent notification. Oregon requires notification and standardized testing, making it the most regulated of the group.
